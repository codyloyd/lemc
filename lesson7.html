Lesson 7 - Chord Progressions

As you have no doubt discovered.. certain arrangements of chords sound better than others.. and regardless of whether or not a progression sounds "GOOD" or "BAD", different progressions create different feelings.

Even using the same chords but changing the order, or changing the rhythm (i.e. how long each chord lasts) can have a significant effect on how a song feels. This lesson doesn't have much new information, instead it involves you playing around with chord progressions to get a feel for what types of sounds you like in your music.

Enter HOOKPAD. Hookpad makes it easy to play around and experiment with chords and do things like change the key or swap out chords on the fly.

  1. go to the hookpad website (linky-link) and spend about 15 minutes playing around with the tool. See how many cool sounding progressions you can create
  2. Either create a new progression (or use one you already made in the previous step) and see if you can translate the chords in hookpad into Renoise.
    • To do this successfully keep track of the Key you are in, then just spell out the chords using the notes from the correct scale.
    • You shouldn't need to worry too much about whether the chords are major or minor so long as you keep to the Key that you're writing in... it should just work out. (for now)
    • If you can manage this, you are ready to move on to the assignment......
